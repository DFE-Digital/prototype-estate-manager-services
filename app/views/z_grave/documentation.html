How we got here

Through an iterative process, we raised the fidelity for the prototype based on each round of research.

Current iteration (iteration 3)

A high fidelity prototype created with the aim to validate the boundary between public facing content and interactions for signed in users.

This prototype is being taken to research with users with low engagement with DfE services in early March 2025.

Iteration 2

A low fidelity prototype created with the aim to validate the information architecture with Operational staff in Estates Management.

Iteration 1

A low fidelity prototype created with the aim to validate the information architecture with Strategic staff in Estates Management.

Research conducted

In discovery around 20 UR interviews with Responsible bodies were done in addition to desk based research (other discoveries and internal interviews).

Needs chime with findings from School Account and other research. For example, users need clear and concise instructions on what they must do, they need certainty on when bid processes start/end, they need collaboration with DfE to understand policy intent and to operationalise it.

No UR done with the prototype below itself: it needs a lot of work and iteration, and we expect it would change a lot in that time - but we've tried to copy existing pieces of design which hopefully have been well-researched and used

Business needs

As DfE we need reassurance RBs are on top of their estates, have policies and strategies in place and are implementing DfE's Good Estates Management for Schools (GEMS) guidance.

As DfE we need an up to date understanding of the condition of EEs, so that we can prioritise / target funding. (current DfE condition data is lighter touch / higher level than condition data that RBs have for their own needs)

Hypothesis: RBs want to see what data DfE holds and are happy to provide a more accurate picture of their estate. This info could be used to support any estate funding bids/applications.
