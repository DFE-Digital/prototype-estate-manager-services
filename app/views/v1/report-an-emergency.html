Reporting structural issues or emergencies

Recognising and reporting structural issues early helps prevent risks to students, staff, and visitors.

Schools and responsible bodies should have clear procedures in place for assessing, managing, and reporting serious issues with their buildings.

Responsibilities for managing risks

It is the responsibility of schools and responsible bodies to:

  • conduct regular inspections to identify potential issues early
  • take immediate action to mitigate risks where necessary
  • ensure compliance with health and safety regulations
  • report serious issues through the appropriate channel

When to report

You should report structural issues or emergencies when:

  • there is an immediate risk to health and safety, including structural failure or severe damage
  • the issue affects the safe operation of the school, such as major flooding, fire damage or severe weather-related damage
  • urgent repairs are needed to keep the building safe, functional and compliant
  • building conditions need improvement, including roof or window repairs, structural damage, or refurbishing worn- out facilities
  • energy efficiency upgrades are necessary, such as better insulation or energy-saving lighting
  • health and safety concerns need addressing, including asbestos removal, security enhancements, or upgrading new locks
