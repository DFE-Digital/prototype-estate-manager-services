Plan your estate maintenance and improvements

The estate buildings are an important and valuable long-term asset. They may also be open to the public as community facilities.

You should plan ongoing maintenance and look at the key areas in your estate that need improvement.

Read our estate management standards framework for more information.

Getting started with your estates maintenance plan

Having a plan for your estates maintenance and improvements helps you manage the school's land and buildings effectively. This keeps a school's estate safe and well maintained, as well as supports educational needs and goals. It ensures you have the right plans, processes and documents in place.

  • Planning and organising your estate resources
  • Benefits of strategic estate management (PDF)
  • Fundamentals of good estate management
  • Strategic estate management

Health and safety of your estate

You should ensure the safety of pupils, staff and visitors by managing your statutory responsibilities and other health and safety issues in the school estate to minimise health and safety issues.

Good estate management includes managing and maintaining the health and safety responsibilities of school estate.

  • Asbestos guidance
  • Health and safety guidance
  • Maintaining your estate

Sustainability in your estate

Good estate management should help you identify opportunities to make better use of your estate by:

  • enhancing the natural environment with nature-based solutions
  • allowing local community groups, schools or colleges to use available indoor or outdoor spaces - initiatives such as The Linking Network can help with building connections and strengthening communities
  • utilising roof space or unused land for renewable energy sources
