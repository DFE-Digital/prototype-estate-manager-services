To manage your school estate effectively, you need a clear understanding of its condition.

This will help you plan strategically, maintain and improve your buildings.

Estate management responsibilities include:

  • carrying out regular condition surveys
  • completing statutory inspections and assessments
  • managing materials like asbestos safely
  • ensuring compliance with health, safety, and fire regulations
  • keeping an up-to-date asset register
  • understanding legal interests and land ownership

Read our estate management standards framework for more information.

Commission a condition report

To understand the physical condition of buildings in your estate, you can commission your own a condition survey. Condition surveys help you:

  • identify what work is needed to maintain the estate
  • consider how much works might cost
  • prioritise work within available funds
  • understand if the nature of the buildings change

You can use DfE approved suppliers do this.

Download your Condition Data Collection (CDC) reports

CDC2 data will provide a comprehensive picture of the condition of the school estate in England. CDC2 will help:

  • provide DfE with an up-to-date evidence base
  • inform future condition funding allocations
  • help prioritise investment in the school estate to where it is most needed
  • identify school buildings for inclusion in rebuilding programmes
  • responsible bodies, and their schools, benefit from capital allocations based on a consistent assessment across key elements of condition need
  • DfE target aggregated funding allocation at responsible body level

Learn more about the Condition Data Collection 2 (CDC2) programme.
