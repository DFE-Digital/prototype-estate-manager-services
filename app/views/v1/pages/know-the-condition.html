Understand the condition of your estate

To help you manage your estate effectively, you should have policies and procedures in place to collect information about the condition of the estate and its land, buildings and property.

This will help you:

  • plan strategically for the long term
  • maintain and improve your estate
  • explore funding options

Read our estate management standards framework for more information.

Commission a survey report

Understanding the condition of the estate helps you make informed decisions about its maintenance and helps with budgeting and estate planning.

This is usually done by carrying out condition surveys.

Condition surveys are technical assessments and should be done by qualified building professionals.

They provide systematic, uniform and objective information on the state of the buildings.

To understand the condition of your estate, you can commission your own survey.

You can use DfE approved suppliers to do this.

Download your Condition Data Collection (CDC) reports

CDC2 data will provide a comprehensive picture of the condition of the school estate in England. CDC2 will help:

  • provide DfE with an up-to-date evidence base
  • inform future condition funding allocations
  • help prioritise investment in the school estate to where it is most needed
  • identify school buildings for inclusion in rebuilding programmes
  • responsible bodies, and their schools, benefit from capital allocations based on a consistent assessment across key elements of condition need
  • DfE target aggregated funding allocation at responsible body level
